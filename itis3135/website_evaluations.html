Website Evaluations

This page looks at two websites and checks how they follow the CRAP design principles. CRAP stands for Contrast, Repetition, Alignment, and Proximity. These are simple rules that help make a site easy to use and nice to look at.

Apple

Apple’s website shows their products with a clean and modern design. The layout is simple, the visuals are bold, and it reflects Apple’s branding.

  • Contrast: Good use of white space and bold visuals. Text stands out clearly.
  • Repetition: Fonts, colors, and layouts are the same across the site.
  • Alignment: Has grid-based layout keeps everything balanced and easy to follow.
  • Proximity: Related content is grouped together, making information easy to find.

The Big Ugly Website

The Big Ugly Website is intentionally designed to look terrible. It’s cluttered, chaotic, and makes it hard for users to focus on important content.

  • Contrast: Colors clash, making text and elements difficult to read.
  • Repetition: Styles are inconsistent, different fonts and colors everywhere.
  • Alignment: Elements feel randomly placed with no structure.
  • Proximity: Information is everywhere on the page, and related items are not grouped together.
